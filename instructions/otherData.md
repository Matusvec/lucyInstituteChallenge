Using Census Data

Website: https://data.census.gov/

The general steps for using the Census website:
Step 1. Go to https://data.census.gov/
Step 2. Click Advanced Search
Step 3. Filter data by location, time, and topic type(s)
Step 4. Search for data in your chosen filters
Step 5. Select the table that best suits your needs
Step 6. Edit the table as needed

a. Adding more data or removing unnecessary data

Step 7. Export data

Step 1:

When first going to the website, you’ll be met with a search bar akin to Google. You can try
typing in search queries (i.e. “poverty in all Indiana counties in 2012”) here to search the
entire census site. However, it’s likely more helpful to click the advanced search option.

Step 2:
Advanced Search will give you a filter bar
on the left hand of the screen which
allows you to click through and select
the filters and variables you’re looking
for directly.
You can search for variables directly in
the search bar or keep expanding
parameters by clicking the blue arrows
next to them.

Step 3:

There are 5 major categories you can search through:
1. Geographies
a. The place you want your data to be about.
This can be anything from zipcodes, states,
counties, to census tracts.

2. Topics
a. This option allows you to choose what
topics or major variables you’re looking for
data on.
3. Surveys
a. The Census Bureau includes data from numerous national surveys including
the American Community Survey and a plethora of Economic Surveys.
Filtering by survey may not be the most useful option unless you are already
familiar with all the different surveys.

4. Years
a. The year(s) the data should be from.
5. Codes
a. Codes is for filtering by material or industry codes. You likely will not need to
use Codes.

Clicking on Topics will bring up the major types of data the
Census collects. If you’re unsure how to find a specific
variable, it’s likely that it can be found by drilling down in the
Topics section.

When you
apply
filters,
you’ll see
them pop
up in the
top left.

Searching for a term will bring up any filter that includes that term, sorted by the category.
In the case of searching poverty for Indiana in 2012, you’ll see 3 options pop up that are
likely to be what you want (Income and Poverty, Poverty, and Official Poverty Measure).

Step 4:
After you select all the filters you want, click the Search button on the bottom right.

This will bring up a page showing all the Tables of interest:

Step 5:
From here, you can click a Table name (i.e. S1701) and the right side of the screen will show
a snippet of the spreadsheet.

Step 6:
Clicking More Tools gives you options to change or export your
data.
To add more variables of interest, click Topics, Geos, or Year.
Note that if you filtered your data in Geos or Topics, then you’ll
want to delete those filters to show all the additional variables
you could add in.

Click Columns to rearrange the column order or to de-select
columns from showing.

Margin of Error is a toggle only – by default it will be selected to
show the Margin of Error in the data as separate columns.

Step 7:

To export your data you can choose Excel, CSV, or ZIP. Clicking
any of these options starts a download of the data set you’ve
created.

Using CDC Wonder Data

Website: https://wonder.cdc.gov/

The general steps for using the CDC Wonder website:
Step 1. Go to https://wonder.cdc.gov/
Step 2. Enter a search term
Step 3. Filter data by location, time, and topic type(s)
Step 4. Select the table that best suits your needs
Step 5. Edit the table as needed

Step 1:

The CDC Wonder site has a few options for searching records and gathering data. While
you could search through the WONDER system/Topics/ or A-Z index, it might take a qhile to
find what you need.

Step 2:
Enter a search term, like “mortality”, “cause of death”,
“overdose” and click search.

Step 3:
Specify some filter terms (like a location and year) for
your search term on the left side. You can click on the
“or” buttons to change them to filter to include (“and”)
or exclude (“and not”) your queries.

Step 4: From the list of tables shown, select the one that
you’d like to search within. You’ll then be prompted to agree to the CDC’s data use
agreement. After you agree, a new query screen will show up.
Step 5:
Depending on your search, there’s around a few more subsections that allow you to better
refine your search. For example, the first subsection allows you to organize the table layout
by additional queries such as location, year, or age.

The next few filters go from over-arching (location, demographics, time) to data set specific
(i.e. place of death, cause of death).

The final subsection shows how you’d like your data to show. Click on the Export Results
option to have the data downloaded to a file.

Once you’ve selected your filters, click Send.

You’ll now be brought to the Results tab. Feel free to check out the other tabs to see a Map
or Chart of your data as well!

If your results aren’t what you were looking for, you can click on the Request Form tab to
redo your filters and try again.
